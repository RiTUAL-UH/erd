 Did you know that a Serb was the chief of a Native American Indian tribe? Born to an Indian mother and Serbian father, Richard M. Milanovich grew up in poverty in Palm Springs, living in a shack and receiving government handouts of surplus food. He served as in infantryman in the United States Army from 1960-1963, and later returned to the Agua Caliente Band of Cahuilla Indians Reservation to engage in tribal politics. The first function Milanovich had in the tribal hierarchy was the post of the tribal secretary, but eventually he was elected Chairman of the Tribal Council in 1984. He won the Indian elections thanks to his plan of agrarian reform in the territory of Indian reservations. Under his leadership, Cahuilla Indians regained a large portion of land that belonged to them and on their territory they began opening hotels and casinos that brought them great profit. Through his vision and leadership, the Agua Caliente Band became the most prosperous group of Indians in the United States
